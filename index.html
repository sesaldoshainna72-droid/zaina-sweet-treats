HISTORY

Zaina Sweet Treats was founded in 2020 it had been more than a bakery it was a living archive of flavors, memories, and traditions passed down through the women of the Servilla family. The history began over a century ago in a small coastal town, where Zainas great grandmother, Laleh, baked her first honey date cake using ingredients she could gather from the nearby market stalls. Her cakes quickly became legendary, not only for their delicate sweetness but for the way they brought the community together during celebrations, weddings, and seaside festivals. When Lalehs daughter, Marjorie, inherited the recipes, she added her own twist infusing spices from the familys travels, like saffron and cardamom, creating the now famous Golden Dawn Cake, which became a symbol of hope during difficult times.

THE LIFE OF THE OWNER:

Marjorie, the dedicated founder of Zainas Sweet Treats, built her business from a simple love for baking and a desire to share the joy of homemade sweetness with her community. Growing up surrounded by the comforting aromas of family made delicacies, she developed a passion for creating desserts that not only taste good but also bring people together. With determination and creativity, Marjorie transformed her home based hobby into a cherished local brand, crafting cakes, pastries, and Filipino treats made with heartfelt care.

THE SUCCESS OF THE BUSINESS :

The success of the owner of Zainas Sweet Treats has quickly grown from a home based passion project into a thriving and beloved dessert brand under the creative leadership of its owner, Marjorie Mangabat. Known for premium ingredients, unique flavors, and beautifully crafted custom treats, the business has built a strong and loyal following through glowing reviews, word of mouth support, and Marjorie warm, personalized approach to customer service. With its expanding menu, rising online presence, and unwavering commitment to quality, Zainas Sweet Treats continues to stand out in the dessert world proving that Marjories dedication and talent have made success truly sweet.

THE CHALLENGES MET BY THE FOUNDER:

Despite the growing success of Zainas Sweet Treats, founder Marjorie Mangabat faced numerous challenges on her path to building the thriving dessert brand it is today. Starting with limited resources and balancing her time between family, work, and her passion for baking, Marjorie often worked late nights perfecting recipes and fulfilling orders from her home kitchen. She navigated the difficulties of sourcing quality ingredients, managing increasing demand, and learning the business side of entrepreneurship all without formal training. Yet her determination, creativity, and commitment to her customers allowed her to push through every obstacle. These challenges not only strengthened her vision but also shaped Zainas Sweet Treats into the successful and heartfelt brand it is now.

HOW I SEE THE BUSINESS AFTER 10 YEARS:

In 10 years, Zainas Sweet Treats, owned by Marjorie Mangabat and inspired by her one daughter, will be a well-loved and successful dessert business known for its delicious flavors, beautiful designs, and warm customer service. Marjorie may have a bigger shop or even a few branches where people come not just for the treats but for the happy, cozy feeling her brand gives. The menu will grow with new and exciting flavors, the online presence will be strong with many loyal customers, and the business might even offer nationwide delivery. Overall, the future of Zainas Sweet Treats looks bright, growing, and full of sweet success.

HOW THE GOVERNMENT OF THE PHILIPPINES HELP HER BUSINESS:

The Philippine government helped Zainas Sweet Treats grow by providing support for small businesses, such as easier business registration, access to training programs, and seminars from DTI that helped improve skills in marketing, budgeting, and product quality. Government programs also offered low-interest loans and financial assistance that made it possible to buy equipment and supplies. Local government units promoted small businesses through community events and markets, giving Zainas Sweet Treats more customers and exposure. All these efforts helped the business become stronger and more stable over time.
